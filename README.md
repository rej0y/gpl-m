## 4. Source Form Requirement

If you share, publish, migrate, or provide public Access to a GPL-M Project or Derivative Project, you must make its Source Form reasonably available.

You do not need to provide perfect documentation. Minecraft players are not known for that.

However, you must provide enough information that another reasonable player can understand, copy, repair, or modify the Project without having to summon a committee of redstone archaeologists.

Acceptable Source Form may include:

- A schematic
- A world download
- A tutorial
- A labeled build
- Screenshots with explanations
- A written guide
- A server wiki page
- A book and quill
- A Discord post
- Clearly marked signs near the Project

Unacceptable Source Form includes:

- “Figure it out”
- “Ask Steve”
- “It works on my machine”
- “Just watch the pistons”
- A single screenshot taken at night during rain
- A secret explanation available only to the builder’s cat

## 6. In-Game Access

A GPL-M Project must remain reasonably accessible for its intended community use.

You may not place the Project, a Derivative Project, or its main useful function behind a Paywall.

This includes required payment through valuable items, server currency, required “donations,” mandatory guild fees, access rent, trade gates, or other in-game economic barriers.

Optional donations are allowed if they are truly optional.

A donation is truly optional only if a player who gives nothing still receives the same practical Access as a player who gives something.

If the “free” version is unusable, humiliating, hidden behind lava, or guarded by a warden named Customer Support, it is not truly free.

For example:

- You may trade with a GPL-M trading hall.
